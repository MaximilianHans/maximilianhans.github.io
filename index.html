Homepage of Maximilian Hans

I am PhD student of Daniel Kasprowski at the University of Southampton. Before that, I was based in Bonn, working with Peter Teichner. My interests lie primarily in low-dimensional topology. Recently I have been interested in the interaction between homotopy theory and low-dimensional topology, specifically in the form of studying the homotopy type of embedding spaces and Goodwillie-Weiss embedding calculus. I am also interested in the relation between generalisations of the Kervaire-Milnor invariant, Whitney towers, and embedding calculus. Furthermore, the role of the Kervaire-Milnor invariant in the stable classification of 4-manifolds.

Here are some notes I have written covering various topics. For the most part, they do not contain original material. Some of them are still very much work in progress.

  • A Light Bulb Theorem for Multi-Disks.
  • The Kervaire Invariant and a non-smoothable Manifold.
  • The Pontrjagin-Thom Construction.
  • The Birman-Craggs Homomorphisms.
  • The Rokhlin Invariant and Homology Cobordism.
  • Symplectic Representation of the Mapping Class Group.
  • Homotopy Theory as a Cohomology Theory.
